Work

Resume || Email

September 2014, I dropped my Chemical Engineering major and learned how to code for the first time.
October 2014, I attended the Grace Hopper Conference, where I got an internship with Verizon.
October 2014-May 2015, I travelled to every hackathon I could (and in February, helped host my school's first MLH-sanctioned one).
December 2014 I joined CWRU Hacker Society's executive board as PR Chair and began planning CWRU's 2015 Grace Hopper trip.
By the time March 2015 rolled around, I was ready to quit my reliable (albeit deadend) part-time job as a barista to risk pursuing my own private consulting business.

After all the hackathons I attended, I had a pretty strong curiosity for design, desire to create, and (along with a certain penchant for project management) an underlying motivation to share the glittery world of development and code with those around me.
I was lucky enough to meet some like-minded people and have helped create IncubatorCLE along the way.
I most definitely do not know everything, but I've learned quite a lot from juggling a consulting business, hacker society, grace hopper, school, an internship, and the incubator I work for.
My skills lie in communication, design, code, and music.
My interests lie in computer science. #CWRU2017
